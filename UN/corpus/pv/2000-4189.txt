The meeting was called to order at 1.30 p.m.
Expression of sympathy to the Russian Federation
The President: At the outset, I should like, on
behalf of the Council, to express our deepest sympathy
to the Government of the Russian Federation, its
people and the families of the bereaved in connection
with the tragic death of the crew of the submarine
Kursk.
Adoption of the agenda
The agenda was adopted.
The situation concerning the Democratic Republic of
the Congo
Letter dated 14 August 2000 from the
Secretary-General addressed to the President of
the Security Council (S/2000/799)
The President: The Security Council will now
begin its consideration of the item on its agenda. The
Council is meeting in accordance with the
understanding reached in its prior consultations.
Members of the Council have before them a letter
dated 14 August 2000 from the Secretary-General
addressed to the President of the Security Council,
document S/2000/799.
Members of the Council also have before them
document S/2000/823, which contains the text of a
draft resolution prepared in the course of the Council‚Äôs
prior consultations.
It is my understanding that the Council is ready to
proceed to the vote on the draft resolution (S/2000/823)
before it. Unless I hear any objection, I shall put the
draft resolution to the vote now.
There being no objection, it is so decided.
A vote was taken by show of hands.
In favour:
Argentina, Bangladesh, Canada, China, France,
Jamaica, Malaysia, Mali, Namibia, Netherlands,
Russian Federation, Tunisia, Ukraine, United
Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland,
United States of America
The President: There were 15 votes in favour.
The draft resolution has been adopted unanimously as
resolution 1316 (2000).
The Security Council has thus concluded the
present stage of its consideration of the item on its
agenda.
The Security Council will remain seized of the
matter.
The meeting rose at 1.35 p.m.
